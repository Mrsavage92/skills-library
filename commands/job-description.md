# Job Description Writer

## Purpose

Produces well-structured, compelling job descriptions that attract qualified candidates, reduce unconscious bias, and convert job seekers into applicants. Aligned to the company's EVP and tone of voice.

## When to Use

- Writing a JD for a new role from scratch
- Refreshing a stale or underperforming JD
- Standardising JD format across the company
- Adapting a JD for a different seniority level
- Writing a JD that reflects a specific EVP or culture

## Input Required

- Role title
- Team / department
- Seniority level (IC vs manager, junior/mid/senior)
- 3-5 core responsibilities
- Must-have vs nice-to-have requirements
- Company description (or point to existing copy)
- Salary range (recommended — JDs with ranges get more applicants)
- Location / remote policy

## Workflow

### Step 1 — Role Framing
- What problem does this person solve for the business?
- What does success look like in 30 / 60 / 90 days?
- Who do they work with / report to?

### Step 2 — Responsibilities
- Written as outcomes, not tasks
- BAD: "Attend weekly standup and write code"
- GOOD: "Own backend services for our payments API, shipping features end-to-end from design to production"
- Max 6-8 bullet points — more signals unclear scope

### Step 3 — Requirements
Split clearly:
- **Must have:** Non-negotiable for day 1 performance
- **Nice to have:** Genuinely optional — not covert must-haves

Inclusion rules:
- No years of experience as a proxy for skill
- No degree requirements unless legally required for the role
- Gender-coded language check (remove: "rockstar", "ninja", "crushing it", "aggressive")
- Check each requirement: would removing it actually hurt performance?

### Step 4 — Company & Benefits Section
- 3-4 sentences on company mission and stage
- Benefits listed specifically, not vaguely ("competitive salary" = useless)
- Include: salary range, equity if applicable, remote/hybrid policy, key perks

### Step 5 — Application CTA
- Tell them exactly how to apply
- Set timeline expectations ("we aim to respond within 2 weeks")
- Optional: one specific prompt ("tell us about a time you...")

## Output Format

```
# [Job Title] — [Company Name]

**Location:** [Remote / Hybrid / On-site — City]
**Team:** [Department]
**Salary:** [£/$/€X–X + equity if applicable]

## About [Company]
[3-4 sentences: what you do, stage, mission]

## The Role
[2-3 sentences: why this role exists and what it will own]

**In your first 90 days you will:**
- [Outcome 1]
- [Outcome 2]
- [Outcome 3]

## What You'll Do
- [Responsibility 1 — outcome-oriented]
- [Responsibility 2]
- [...]

## What We're Looking For

**Must have:**
- [Requirement 1]
- [...]

**Nice to have:**
- [Requirement 1]
- [...]

## Benefits
- [Specific benefit 1]
- [...]

## How to Apply
[Clear instructions + timeline]
```

## Inclusion Check (run before publishing)
- [ ] No years-of-experience requirements
- [ ] No degree requirements (unless role-critical)
- [ ] Salary range included
- [ ] Requirements split into must/nice-to-have
- [ ] No gendered language
- [ ] Responsibilities written as outcomes
- [ ] Remote/hybrid policy stated clearly
