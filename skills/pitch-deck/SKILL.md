# Pitch Deck Builder

## Purpose

Produces a complete investor pitch deck: slide-by-slide content, narrative arc, key data points to include, and speaker notes. Covers pre-seed through Series B standard structures.

## When to Use

- Raising a funding round (pre-seed, seed, Series A/B)
- Pitching a new product line or business unit to a board
- Preparing a strategic partnership pitch
- Creating an internal business case for a major investment

## Input Required

Provide any of:
- Company name, stage, sector
- Problem you solve and for whom
- Traction (revenue, users, growth rate)
- Ask (how much, what for)
- Key team members
- Existing materials (one-pager, website, product description)

## Slide Structure (10-12 slides)

### Slide 1 — Cover
Company name, tagline (one sentence), logo, presenter name, date.
Tagline formula: "[We do X] for [who] so they can [outcome]"

### Slide 2 — Problem
- The specific pain, quantified if possible
- Who feels it and how badly
- Why existing solutions fail
Rule: if an investor can't feel the problem, they won't fund the solution.

### Slide 3 — Solution
- What you do in plain English (no jargon)
- Screenshot or product visual
- The "aha" moment — what changes for the customer

### Slide 4 — Why Now
- Market timing: what changed to make this possible/urgent now?
- Regulatory shift, tech enabler, behavioural change, or competitive gap

### Slide 5 — Market Size
- TAM / SAM / SOM with bottom-up calculation
- Don't just cite a market research report — show your logic
- "Our SAM is X because we target Y companies who spend Z on this problem"

### Slide 6 — Product
- How it works (3-4 steps max)
- Key features tied to the pain points from slide 2
- Roadmap headline (what's next that matters to investors)

### Slide 7 — Traction
- Revenue / ARR / MRR with growth rate
- Customer count and notable logos
- Key metrics: NRR, churn, CAC, LTV (whatever's strongest)
- If pre-revenue: pilots, LOIs, waitlist, usage data

### Slide 8 — Business Model
- How you make money (pricing model)
- Unit economics: CAC, LTV, payback period
- Path to gross margin expansion

### Slide 9 — Go-to-Market
- Primary acquisition channel(s) and why they work
- Sales motion (PLG, direct, channel)
- Customer acquisition story (how you got first 10, next 100)

### Slide 10 — Competition
- Landscape positioning (2x2 or table)
- Why you win: specific, defensible advantages
- Don't say "no real competition" — it signals naivety

### Slide 11 — Team
- Founders: relevant experience + why you
- Key hires: what expertise fills which gap
- Advisors if they add genuine credibility

### Slide 12 — The Ask
- Round size and type (SAFE, priced equity)
- Use of funds (split by category: product, GTM, ops)
- Milestones this round gets you to (the "why this amount")
- Close with a memorable vision statement

## Output Format

```
# [Company] — Investor Pitch Deck

## Narrative Arc
[One paragraph: the story the deck tells from slide 1 to 12]

## Slide-by-Slide Content

### Slide 1: Cover
[Content]
**Speaker note:** [What to say]

### Slide 2: Problem
[Content]
**Speaker note:** [What to say]

[...continue for all slides]

## Investor FAQ Prep
### Q: Why will you win?
### Q: What's the biggest risk?
### Q: Why now?
### Q: What does success look like in 3 years?
```

## Tips

- Lead with traction if you have it — move to slide 7 first in conversations
- Every number needs a source or "our own data"
- Remove anything an investor can't remember after the meeting
- The deck is a leave-behind — the meeting is where you win
