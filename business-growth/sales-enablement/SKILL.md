# Sales Enablement

## Overview

Sales enablement equips revenue teams with the content, tools, and knowledge to close deals faster. This skill covers playbook design, competitive intelligence, objection handling, demo scripting, and sales process optimisation.

## When to Use

- Building or improving a sales playbook
- Creating competitive battle cards
- Designing objection handling frameworks
- Writing demo scripts or discovery guides
- Improving ramp time for new sales hires
- Analysing win/loss patterns

## Quick Start

```
# Build a sales playbook
Share your ICP, product, and competitors → structured sales playbook

# Competitive battle card
Name a competitor → battle card with positioning, objections, landmines

# Objection handling
List top 5 objections you hear → handling guide with talk tracks
```

## Sales Playbook Structure

### 1. ICP & Personas
- Ideal Customer Profile (industry, size, tech stack, budget range)
- Economic Buyer, Champion, Technical Evaluator personas
- Pain points, motivations, success metrics per persona

### 2. Discovery Framework (MEDDIC)
- **M**etrics — what does success look like in numbers?
- **E**conomic Buyer — who controls the budget?
- **D**ecision Criteria — how will they evaluate?
- **D**ecision Process — what are the steps to close?
- **I**dentify Pain — what is the cost of inaction?
- **C**hampion — who will sell internally for us?

### 3. Demo Script Template
```
Opening (2 min): Confirm pain, agenda, success criteria
Discovery recap (3 min): "Based on what you told me..."
Demo flow (20 min):
  → Pain → Feature → Benefit → Proof (repeat x3)
  → Show the "wow moment" early
Close (5 min): Next steps, stakeholders, timeline
```

### 4. Objection Handling Guide

| Objection | Root Cause | Response Framework |
|-----------|------------|-------------------|
| "Too expensive" | Budget or value mismatch | ROI calculation + proof |
| "We built it in-house" | Sunk cost + control | TCO comparison + roadmap |
| "Not the right time" | No urgency | Cost of inaction + deadline |
| "Need to talk to IT/Legal" | Process blocker | Accelerate with champion |
| "Evaluating competitors" | FOMO or price anchoring | Battle card + differentiation |

### 5. Competitive Battle Card Template
```
## [Competitor Name]

**Their Pitch:** One sentence summary of their positioning

**Where We Win:**
- Feature/capability we're better at
- Price/value advantage
- Integration/ecosystem advantage

**Where They Win:**
- Be honest about gaps

**Landmines to Plant:**
- Questions to ask that expose their weaknesses

**Talk Track:**
"When you're evaluating [Competitor], make sure to ask them about X..."

**Proof Points:**
- Customer win against this competitor
- Third-party review comparison
```

## Win/Loss Analysis Framework
1. Tag deals as Won/Lost in CRM
2. Record reason (price, competition, timing, fit, champion left)
3. Monthly review: pattern analysis by reason, rep, segment, deal size
4. Quarterly: update playbook, battle cards, and training based on patterns

## Sales Ramp Programme (90-day)
| Week | Focus |
|------|-------|
| 1–2 | Product deep-dive, customer calls, CRM setup |
| 3–4 | Shadow 5 discovery calls, 5 demos |
| 5–6 | Run discovery with manager shadow |
| 7–8 | Full cycle with coaching |
| 9–12 | Full quota ramping, weekly 1:1 coaching |

## Key Metrics
- **Win rate** by stage, segment, rep
- **Sales cycle length** — days from opportunity created to close
- **Average deal size** — track trend over time
- **Pipeline coverage** — 3× quota minimum
- **Ramp time** — days to first deal, days to full quota attainment
